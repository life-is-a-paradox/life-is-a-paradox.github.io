Life is a Paradox

A Poem About Contradictions, Suffering, and the Hope Beyond

Life is a paradox. To some, it feels like paradise; to others, it burns like hell. For some, life is all they hold onto — for others, death is all they crave. Perspective shapes it, and situation defines it. To me, life is a test, and death is not the end. Be aware — there is something beyond the paradox. To some, that thought is comforting; to others, it is unsettling. Those who feel paradise in this world may lose their way — though not all do. Those who suffer through hell may find the true path — though not all will. For truth is not guaranteed by pain, nor is it denied by ease. The paradox remains… but beyond it, something greater waits.

By Monir Islam

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the purpose of life?

The purpose of life is to worship and obey God (Allah) through faith. But these are only the first steps — the ultimate goal is to try my best to earn the satisfaction of my Creator.

Why is life called a paradox?

Because it is full of contradictions — suffering and joy, life and death. This paradox points to something greater beyond.

Who is the true Creator?

The true Creator is the One and Only God ( ALLAH ), who gave meaning to life and created everything with purpose.

What does Islam say about the meaning of life?

Islam teaches that the meaning of life is submission to the Creator, Allah, by worshipping and obey God (Allah) through faith. But these are only the first steps — the ultimate goal is to try my best to earn the satisfaction of my Creator.

How can logic help us find the true Creator?

Logic shows that a design could not exist without a designer and the universe could not exist without a Creator. Yet the Creator is beyond full human logic, since logic itself was created by Him.
